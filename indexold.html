    • ✨ Fully theme-safe and Bootstrap-powered
    • 🎯 Ideal for focused content without page clutter
    • 🧼 Dismissible, accessible, and joyfully modular

    Use them to deliver wisdom, warnings, or whimsical nonsense—your call.

    🧠 Contributor Wisdom
    • ✨ Feature, not a bug
    • 🧪 Experimental joy
    • 🧼 Clean code, clean conscience
    🧩 Modal Snippet: Deploy with Drama
    • Replace #modalMystery with a unique ID for each modal. This ensures buttons and modals don’t clash.
    • Update the button label (e.g. 🕵️ Reveal the Secret) to reflect what the modal reveals—tips, updates, memes, etc.
    • Change the modal title inside <h5 id="modalMysteryLabel"> to match your content theme.
    • Drop your content into the .card-body—lists, onboarding notes, changelogs, or contributor shout-outs.
    • Duplicate the whole block for multiple modals—just make sure each ID is unique!

    🚨 Alert Snippets: Style Your Signals
    • Choose your alert type: Swap alert-success, alert-warning, alert-danger, etc. to match the tone—success, caution, error, info, or themed nudges.
    • Update the message: Change the bolded lead-in (e.g. <strong>Success!</strong>) and the follow-up text to suit your context.
    • Make it dismissible: Keep the btn-close and fade show classes to allow contributors to close the alert manually.

    Tabs

    Tabs are your modular magic wand for taming content chaos. They let you group related info into neat, clickable sections—ideal for onboarding guides, role-based layouts, or showcasing multiple dohickies without overwhelming the page.

    • 🧭 Great for navigation between related content blocks
    • 🎨 Theme-safe and responsive—works beautifully across microsites
    • 🧱 Fully modular—just update tab labels and content panes
    • 🧼 Keeps pages clean, scannable, and contributor-friendly

    Use tabs to guide contributors through layered content, toggle between roles, or showcase feature variants—all with clarity and charm.

    Content for Tab 01A. Could be onboarding tips, contributor shout-outs, or a haiku about modularity.

    Content for Tab 01B. Maybe a changelog. Maybe a list of things that aren’t bugs.

    🗂 Tab Snippet Panel: Click Between Worlds
    • Change tab labels: Update Tab 01A, Tab 01B, etc. to reflect your content—roles, features, onboarding steps, or whimsical categories.
    • Update content panes: Drop your content inside each .tab-pane—text, images, dohickies, or contributor shout-outs.
    • Ensure unique IDs: Each tab button and pane must have a matching, unique ID pair (e.g. tabBtn01a ↔ tabPane01a).
    • Set the default tab: Add active to the tab button and show active to the corresponding pane you want visible on load.
    • Stack or nest tabs: You can place tabs inside modals, cards, or even other tabs—just keep the IDs tidy and scoped.
    • Optional: Add icons, emojis, or tooltips to tab labels for extra clarity or charm.

    Breadcrumbs

    Breadcrumbs are your gentle guide through the microsite maze. They show contributors where they are, how they got there, and how to hop back—without ever feeling lost. Perfect for layered layouts, role-based navigation, or multi-step onboarding flows.

    • 🧭 Helps contributors orient themselves within nested content
    • 🔗 Provides quick links back to parent pages or sections
    • 🎨 Theme-safe and accessible—works across microsite palettes
    • 🧱 Modular and easy to drop into headers, cards, or dohickies

    Whether you're guiding someone from “Home → Onboarding → Role Cards” or “Library → Snippets → Tabs”, breadcrumbs keep the journey clear and joyful.

    1. Home
    2. Catalogue
    3. Statutory Training
    🍞 Breadcrumb Snippet Panel: Trace the Trail
    • Update the trail: Change each <li> label to reflect your page hierarchy—e.g. Home → Library → Tabs or Dashboard → Role Cards → Editor.
    • Control interactivity: Use onclick="showPage('X')" or href="..." depending on whether you're using JavaScript routing or standard links.
    • Mark the current page: Add active and aria-current="page" to the final breadcrumb item to signal where the user is.
    • Theme-safe by default: No extra styling needed—just drop it into any microsite layout and it’ll harmonise beautifully.
    • Optional: Add icons, emojis, or role-based labels to make the trail more expressive (e.g. 🏠 Home, 🧑‍💼 Role Cards).
    
<nav aria-label="breadcrumb">
  <ol class="breadcrumb">
    <li class="breadcrumb-item"><a onclick="showPage('home')">Home</a></li>
    <li class="breadcrumb-item"><a onclick="showPage('CC')">Catalogue</a></li>
    <li class="breadcrumb-item active" aria-current="page">Statutory Training</li>
  </ol>
</nav>

    🔍 Mermaid Font Size & Complexity

    Mermaid diagrams are brilliant for visualising flows, but they do have a few quirks—especially when things get big, bold, and beautifully complex. Here’s what contributors should know:

    • 📏 Font size may shrink automatically: Mermaid tries to fit the entire diagram into the available space. If your chart is large or dense, it may reduce the font size to avoid overflow.
    • 🧠 Complexity affects readability: Charts with lots of nodes, embedded HTML, or deep branching structures are more likely to trigger auto-scaling. Even if you set a larger font size, Mermaid may override it.
    • 🧩 Break big charts into chunks: For better readability and contributor joy, split complex flows into smaller Mermaid blocks. You can group them by theme, role, or phase—like “Manager Flow,” “Contributor Journey,” or “Mascot Trials.”
    • 🎨 Style your content safely: Use a custom CSS class like .flow-box to control font size, spacing, and layout inside Mermaid nodes. This gives you more control than Mermaid’s built-in settings.
    • 🔍 Zoom with CSS: You can apply transform: scale(1.2) to the Mermaid container to visually enlarge the whole diagram. This is great for accessibility mode or presentation views.

    Remember: clarity beats complexity. If your diagram starts feeling like a spaghetti monster, break it into themed chunks and let your mascot guide the way!
