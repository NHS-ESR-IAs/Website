Accessibility Flag

Accessibility flags are your microsite’s promise of inclusion—perfect for highlighting features that support diverse contributor needs. They bring visibility to thoughtful design choices and help contributors feel seen, supported, and empowered.

  • 🔍 Great for surfacing keyboard navigation, screen reader support, high contrast themes, and skip links
  • 🧱 Modular and drop-in ready for cards, dashboards, or onboarding flows
  • 🎨 Theme-safe and expressive—styled to harmonise across microsites
  • 🧼 Keeps accessibility features visible, scannable, and contributor-friendly

Use them to say “This section supports keyboard navigation,” “Screen reader labels are active,” or “Skip links are available for fast navigation”—whatever signals thoughtful, inclusive scaffolding.

♿ Accessibility: This section supports keyboard navigation and screen readers.
♿ Accessibility Flag Panel: Signal Inclusive Support
  • Change the message: Edit the text to highlight specific accessibility features—e.g. keyboard navigation, screen reader support, high contrast themes, or skip links.
  • Swap the emoji: Replace ♿ with 🦮, 🧏, 🔊, or any expressive icon that suits your accessibility tone.
  • Style it safely: Use theme-safe classes like accessibility-flag, border, bg-light, or rounded to harmonise with your microsite.
  • Make it modular: Drop it into cards, modals, or onboarding flows—accessibility flags help contributors feel seen and supported.
  • Optional: Add links to accessibility docs, toggle buttons, or dohickies that activate inclusive features.
